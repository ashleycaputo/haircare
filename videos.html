Dutch Braids Tip: Make sure that your hair is damp before starting. When doing a dutch braid, I usually start with my right hand, and take the pieces under rather than over (you take the pieces over in a french braid).

Neat Bun Tip: Be sure to start with a slick high ponytail and use a strong hairspray. I recommend the Fructis Sleek and Shine (which has a #4 on the can).

Curled Half Up-Half Down Tip: Use a curling wand rather than a curling iron. Using a curling wand will give you loose curls that look more natural.

French Twist Tip: Slick your hair back with some hair jell and use ballet hair pins rather than bobby pins. Ballet hair pins are easier to put into your hair and have a better hold than bobby pins.
